The NFL Has Changed

No sport is static. The NFL is no exception. Through the last decade the NFL has undergone a transformation unlike any sport has seen in our lifetime. In 2008 teams averaged a paltry 3,380 passing yards a season. By 2019 they averaged 3,759. The top teams in the NFL can easily throw for nearly 5,000 yards a season. At the same time, rushing yards have stayed the same or even slightly declined. Intuitively, this might lead one to believe that passing has become an increasingly influential part of the game. However, through a deep statistical analysis of data over the last two decades of the sport we have uncovered that while passing numbers have increased, a teams ability to throw the ball is not the chief determining factor in success. Rushing attempts, turnovers, peanlties, all take precedence over even the first passing statistic. Our findings are covered in more detail in our white paper which can be found under the "about" tab on the banner. In addition to finding which statistical categories were most important to victory we also built a robust neural network that is capable of determining the expected number of wins for a team given their annual stats. These two models are strong proof that the more things change the more they stay the same. Teams that can establish the run are more likely to win more games.
